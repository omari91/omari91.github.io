The Journey of a Hybrid Innovator

From Kitale's curiosity to Berlin's innovation – Engineering with Purpose

Who I Am

I'm Clifford Ondieki, a technical specialist who believes true innovation springs from a deep understanding of systems – whether in power grids or human connections.

My path from the vibrant communities in Kitale, Kenya, to the dynamic landscape of Berlin has been a continuous journey of solution development and finding purpose. Every challenge was a reorientation towards growth, every setback a lesson in resilience that now shapes my approach to technical architecture and team leadership.

As I evolve, I know that for me, building systems will not just be about efficiency – but about building bridges between people, purpose, and possibilities.

From Kitale to Europe – where cliffs become stairs, each step carved with quiet strength. 🐐

The Spark: Defining Moments

The Radio That Changed Everything

Kitale, Kenya - Age 12

It started with a broken radio in our family home in Kitale. While other children played outside, I was fascinated by the complex circuits inside. This moment of disassembling and understanding how each component contributes to the overall system – that was my first experience with what I now call "Engineering Empathy."

It wasn't just about the repair, but about realizing that every system, no matter how complex, has a purpose and every component plays a role. This principle now guides everything from SaaS architectures to team leadership.

The University Project That Taught Me Failure

JKUAT, Kenya - 2015

During my electrical engineering studies, I led a project on power grid optimization that initially failed spectacularly. We had the technical knowledge but overlooked the human factor – how the solution would actually be implemented by people in communities.

This failure taught me that "failure is redirection" – a core principle that now shapes my approach to innovation. It's not about avoiding mistakes, but about learning so quickly and thoroughly that every setback becomes a strategic advantage.

The Decision to Connect Worlds

Nairobi to Berlin - 2025

The move to Berlin was not just a career step, but a conscious decision to be a bridge between the community-oriented problem-solving from Kenya and the innovation-driven tech ecosystem of Europe. I realized that my added value lies not only in technical expertise but in a unique perspective on solution development.

Overcoming & Development

Finding the Balance

One of my biggest challenges was balancing university studies, work responsibilities, and leadership roles without burning out. I tried to excel in electrical engineering studies while working part-time and leading student organizations.

This taught me what I now call "Intelligent Laziness" – the art of understanding systems so deeply that you find the one change that solves ten problems. It's not about working harder, but about systemic intelligence.

Cultural Navigation

Settling in Berlin brought unique challenges – not only language and cultural differences, but also the art of communicating my Kenyan approach to community building and problem-solving in a different cultural context.

This experience deepened my understanding that cultural diversity is not just desirable – it is a competitive advantage. My Kenyan heritage emphasizes community and cooperation, principles that create stronger teams and more sustainable solutions.
