Enabling Equitable EV Charger Deployment in Berlin with Multi-Objective Geospatial Optimization

Currently ongoing data-driven geospatial optimization pipeline to balance coverage and spatial equity in Berlin's EV charging infrastructure.

The Challenge

Public Electric Vehicle (EV) charging infrastructure in Germany is largely demand-driven, reinforcing spatial inequities by favoring central districts and leaving peripheral areas with higher congestion and reduced access. This creates a "chicken-and-egg" problem, where lack of infrastructure suppresses EV adoption in underserved areas, justifying continued underinvestment.

Our Data-Driven Solution

We implemented a multi-objective NSGA-II model in Python to balance coverage and spatial equity, minimizing the district-level Gini coefficient of BEVs per charger. This approach yields different Pareto-optimal deployment strategies for urban planners, directly addressing infrastructure placement under complex geospatial and socio-economic constraints.
